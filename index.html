Canada Goose

In a pattern called “assortative mating” by biologists, geese of both sexes tend to choose mates of a similar size.

  • Scientific Name: Branta canadensis
  • Average Length: 75 to 110 cm
  • Average Lifespan: 10 – 24 years (In the wild)
  • Habitat: Varied

The Canada goose is a large wild goose species with a black head and neck, white patches on the face, and a brown body. Like most geese, it is primarily herbivorous and normally migratory. It tends to be found on or close to fresh water.
